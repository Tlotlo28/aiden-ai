            They Started With Nothing.
            Look at Them Now.

            15 real people from across the globe — including right here in Africa. Real ideas. Real results. Your story could be next.

            Strive Masiyiwa
            🇿🇼 Econet Wireless
            Fought the Zimbabwean government in court for 5 years just for the right to start a mobile network. With no government backing and near-zero resources, he built Econet into a multi-billion dollar telecom operating across 15+ African countries.
            Started with: A court battle & unshakeable belief
            Aliko Dangote
            🇳🇬 Dangote Group
            Started by selling sweets and groundnuts as a child in Nigeria. Borrowed the equivalent of $3,000 from his uncle to trade commodities. Today the Dangote Group is Africa's largest industrial conglomerate spanning cement, sugar, oil and more.
            Started with: A $3,000 loan & a trading stall
            Bethlehem Tilahun Alemu
            🇪🇹 SoleRebels
            Grew up in Addis Ababa's poorest neighbourhood. With no investors, no loans, and no connections, she started making footwear by hand from recycled tyres in 2004. SoleRebels became Africa's first globally certified fair trade footwear company, now sold in 55 countries.
            Started with: Recycled tyres & handcraft skills
            Rapelang Rabana
            🇿🇦 Rekindle Learning
            Born in Botswana, raised in South Africa, she co-founded her first tech company at 22 while still a student at UCT. Later founded Rekindle Learning, a mobile learning platform that transformed corporate training across Africa. Named a WEF Young Global Leader.
            Started with: A student laptop & a vision for Africa
            Ashish Thakkar
            🇺🇬 Mara Group
            Born to Ugandan-Indian parents who fled with nothing during Idi Amin's expulsions. At 15, he started selling second-hand computers from a market stall in Kampala. Today the Mara Group operates across 25 African countries in tech, real estate, and manufacturing.
            Started with: A market stall at age 15
            Nadia Rawjee
            🇿🇦 Naked Insurance
            Co-founded Naked Insurance in South Africa to make car insurance fair and transparent using AI. Built from scratch into one of South Africa's fastest growing insurtech startups, disrupting a billion-rand industry dominated by old-guard companies — proving African founders can out-innovate global giants.
            Started with: A fair idea in an unfair industry
            Howard Schultz
            🇺🇸 Starbucks
            Grew up in Brooklyn public housing. Rejected by investors over 200 times when he pitched the espresso bar concept. Bought the company himself with borrowed money and turned a local coffee bean shop into 35,000+ stores worldwide.
            Started with: Borrowed funds & a rejected idea
            Sara Blakely
            🇺🇸 Spanx
            Was selling fax machines door-to-door when she had the idea to cut the feet off her pantyhose. Started Spanx with $5,000 in savings, no fashion background, no business degree — just a problem she wanted to solve.
            Started with: $5,000 and a simple idea
            Jan Koum
            🇺🇦 WhatsApp
            Immigrated from Ukraine and survived on food stamps in California. Taught himself to code from used textbooks. Built WhatsApp in 2009. Facebook bought it in 2014 for $19 billion, making him one of the world's richest individuals.
            Started with: Food stamps & a library card
            Melanie Perkins
            🇦🇺 Canva
            Was teaching design software at university, frustrated by how complex it was. Pitched Canva to over 100 investors — almost all said no. Launched in 2013 focusing on simplicity. Now used by 150 million people in 190 countries.
            Started with: 100+ rejections & sheer belief
            Kevin Plank
            🇺🇸 Under Armour
            Nearly broke after college. Took $20,000 in savings and racked up $40,000 in credit card debt to make moisture-wicking shirts. First big sale was $17,000 to Georgia Tech. Within years had deals with two dozen NFL teams.
            Started with: $20K savings + maxed credit cards
            John Paul DeJoria
            🇺🇸 Paul Mitchell
            Once homeless, living in his car with his son. Borrowed $700 and cold-called salons to sell hair products door-to-door. That $700 turned into Paul Mitchell Systems, worth over $900 million. He later co-founded Patrón Spirits.
            Started with: $700 and a car to sleep in
            Oprah Winfrey
            🇺🇸 OWN & Harpo Productions
            Born in rural poverty in Mississippi, raised by her grandmother on a farm. Faced incredible adversity but channeled it into storytelling. Started as a local news anchor, eventually built a media empire worth $2.8 billion driven entirely by her voice and vision.
            Started with: A microphone & a story to tell
            Sophia Amoruso
            🇺🇸 Nasty Gal / Girlboss
            Was broke and working as a security guard when she started listing thrift store finds on eBay. Her eye for fashion turned into Nasty Gal — a multi-million dollar brand. Even after a setback, she reinvented herself and built the Girlboss media movement.
            Started with: An eBay account & thrift store finds
            Richard Branson
            🇬🇧 Virgin Group
            Left school at 16 with dyslexia, convinced he'd never succeed academically. Started a student magazine with £100. That magazine became Virgin Records, then Virgin Atlantic, then 400+ companies under the Virgin brand worth billions.
            Started with: £100 & a school dropout certificate
